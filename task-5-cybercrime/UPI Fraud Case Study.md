Case Study: UPI Online Fraud with a Colledge Student.


Whats this UPI Online Fraud?


UPI money fraud is a deceptive practice where scammers exploit the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) system to steal money, usually by tricking users into entering their UPI PIN on a "request money" screen. Common tactics include phishing, fake customer support calls, QR code scams, and screen-sharing apps to gain unauthorized access
Scenario:


Santosh, a first year engineering student once received a call from a spammer asking for his UPI Pin so that 50000 rs can be added to her account.First, he refused to do so and was about to cut his call. But at the end moment the spammer made him to trust that he will actually receive the money and can confirm through an sms.Finally Santosh shared his UPI Pin with the spammer and received a message that your money has been debited to the person's account.Santosh realised soon as he cut the call that it was a fraud and he went to the police station to complain about it


How it typically happens:


1. The Spammer uses fake but a convincing email ID or phone number.
2. He/she easily trick them by their strong communication power and convince them with quite ease.
3. Victim enters his personal accounts credentials or their UPI Pin or OTP.
4. Victim clicks on the fake link thus generating to a fake website.
5. The spammer further misuses these credentials and captures the data immediately.

Who are mostly targetted?


Students,first time netbanking users, senior citizens and all those people who never made online transactions and dont know its rules are the most commonly tagetted people.


Consequences:
1. Unauthorised access to our banking accounts and details
2. Mental and emotional stress to the people and distrust on the upi transactions
3. Financial loss through stolen credentials
